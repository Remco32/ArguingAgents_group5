 Vaccines can save children's lives.
  The American Academy of Pediatrics states that "most childhood vaccines are 90%-99% effective in preventing disease."  According to , a United Nations Foundation partner organization, vaccines save 2.5 million children from preventable diseases every year , which equates to roughly 285 children saved every hour. The Centers for Disease Control (CDC) estimated that 732,000 American children were saved from death and 322 million cases of childhood illnesses were prevented between 1994 and 2014 due to vaccination.  The measles vaccine has decreased childhood deaths from measles by 74%.
