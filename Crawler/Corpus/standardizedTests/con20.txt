 Schools feeling the pressure of NCLB's 100% proficiency requirement are "gaming the system" to raise test scores,
  according to an Arizona State University report in the June 22, 2009, edition of the peer-reviewed .  Low-performing students are "encouraged to stay home" on test days or "counseled to quit or be suspended" before tests are administered. State education boards are "lowering the bar": manipulating exam content or scoring so that tests are easier for students to pass.
