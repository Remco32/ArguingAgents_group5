 A right to health care could make medical services affordable for everyone. 
 According to a 2012 study from , paying for health care is the top financial problem for US households.  According to a peer-reviewed study in , between 2003 and 2013, the cost of family health insurance premiums has increased 80% in the United States.  According to the Kaiser Family Foundation, 26% of Americans report that they or a family member had trouble paying for medical bills in 2012, and 58% reported that they delayed or did not seek medical care due to cost.  According to one estimate of a proposed bill to implement a single-payer health care system in the United States (HR 676), 95% of US households would save money  and every individual in the United States would receive guaranteed access to publically financed medical care.
